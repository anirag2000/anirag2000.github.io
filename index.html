Hi, I’m Anirudh. I am a Computer Science Graduate Student at Rutgers University.

Since the start of my career in 2014, I have more than 8 years of experience in designing and launching products at big tech companies such as Google and Citrix, as well as smaller startups. As a designer, my mission is not only to create beautiful and functional products, but also to build successful businesses. In 2020, amid the global pandemic, I quit Google and embarked on an unusual journey to pursue an MBA degree at Harvard Business School. My seemingly unrelated experience in business and design gave me an analytical and strategic lens on the creative process and places me in a unique position to connect designers and business leaders. My passion lies at the intersection of design, technology, and entrepreneurship. At Google, I co-initiated a quarterly report, called Designed in China, to increase awareness of Chinese tech product trends. The report received rave reviews from over 15k+ internal readership. At Harvard, I served as the Co-president of the Design Club and the Co-Chair of the Harvard-wide annual design conference, HarvardxDesign. I also advise startups on product design and share UX best practices with founders at Harvard. Outside of work, I am a keen traveler and an accomplished artist. I have visited more than 30 countries to date and have had a book of illustrations of my travels in Europe published. For freelance and consulting requests, contact me at anniezhou.red@gmail.com.
