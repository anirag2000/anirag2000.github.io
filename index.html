research.

TCP latency measurement with a passive in-band observer
The purpose of this research is to design a Layer 4 Load Balancer using extended Berkley Filter, that supports a passive in-band observer. The proposed Load Balancer would be able to adapt the request-routing policy using purely local observations of server performance, derived from requests traversing the Load Balancer.
Energy Efficient Approach to Estimate Live Location Using Smartphone’s Barometer
GPS plays a crucial role in our daily lives, providing highly accurate positioning data on demand. However, this comes at the cost of high power consumption. This work uses a smartphone's barometer to estimate the real-time location of a user without relying on real-time GPS. We achieved an average reduction of 83.43% in power consumed by our approach while maintaining an accuracy of 83.14% and an average deviation of 10.839 m compared to real-time GPS coordinates.
Gesture Recognition using Graph Methods
The paper proposes a new and under-explored approach that involves graph based methods for seed marking in image segmentation in the pre-processing phase to separate the hand from it's background, further it is fed as the input to ensemble models to predict and classify the gesture being performed, while allowing the users to be in their natural conditions.
